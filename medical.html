Healing the World Through Innovation

Ancient

Ayurveda and Sushruta Samhita

India gave the world Ayurveda, one of the oldest medical systems. Sushruta, known as the "Father of Surgery," performed complex surgical procedures including cataract surgery and plastic surgery over 2,500 years ago.

1960s

Generic Drug Industry Foundation

India's pharmaceutical industry began its journey to become the "Pharmacy of the World," focusing on affordable generic medicines and making healthcare accessible globally.

1980s

Rotavirus Vaccine Development

Indian scientists contributed significantly to rotavirus vaccine development, helping prevent millions of childhood deaths from diarrheal diseases worldwide.

2000s

Affordable Healthcare Solutions

India pioneered low-cost medical innovations including the Jaipur Foot prosthetic, affordable cardiac stents, and cost-effective surgical procedures, making healthcare accessible to millions.

2014

Jan Aushadhi - Generic Medicine Program

The Pradhan Mantri Bhartiya Janaushadhi Pariyojana made quality generic medicines available at affordable prices, reducing healthcare costs by up to 90% for common medications.

2018

Ayushman Bharat - World's Largest Health Scheme

India launched the world's largest healthcare scheme covering over 500 million people, providing health insurance and promoting preventive healthcare across the nation.

2020

COVID-19 Vaccine Development

India developed indigenous COVID-19 vaccines (Covaxin by Bharat Biotech and Covishield by Serum Institute), becoming the world's largest vaccine producer and exporter during the pandemic.

2021

CoWIN Platform

India's digital vaccination platform CoWIN efficiently managed the world's largest vaccination drive, administering over 2 billion doses and serving as a model for other countries.

2022

Telemedicine and Digital Health

India emerged as a leader in telemedicine and digital health solutions, making healthcare accessible in remote areas through technology-enabled consultations and diagnostics.
