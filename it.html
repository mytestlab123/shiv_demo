Powering the Digital World

1970s

Tata Consultancy Services (TCS)

Founded in 1968, TCS became one of the world's largest IT services companies, pioneering India's software export industry and establishing the foundation for the IT revolution.

1980s

Infosys and the Y2K Opportunity

Indian IT companies like Infosys gained global recognition by solving the Y2K problem for international clients, showcasing India's software expertise to the world.

1990s

Bangalore - Silicon Valley of India

Bangalore emerged as the IT capital of India, attracting global technology companies and fostering a thriving ecosystem of startups and innovation hubs.

2000s

Global IT Services Leader

India became the world's largest exporter of IT services, with companies like TCS, Infosys, Wipro, and HCL establishing global delivery centers worldwide.

2009

Aadhaar - World's Largest Biometric ID

India implemented Aadhaar, the world's largest biometric identification system, serving over 1.3 billion people and enabling digital inclusion across the country.

2016

Digital India Initiative

The Digital India program aimed to transform the country into a digitally empowered society, promoting digital literacy, infrastructure, and governance.

2016

UPI - Revolutionary Payment System

Unified Payments Interface (UPI) revolutionized digital payments in India, processing billions of transactions and becoming a model for digital payment systems globally.

2020

COVID-19 Tech Response

Indian IT sector enabled global remote work during the pandemic, while apps like Aarogya Setu and CoWIN facilitated health monitoring and vaccination drives.

2022

Startup Ecosystem Boom

India became the third-largest startup ecosystem globally with over 100 unicorns, fostering innovation in fintech, edtech, healthtech, and e-commerce.
