India's Journey to the Stars

1975

Aryabhata - India's First Satellite

Named after the ancient Indian mathematician, Aryabhata was India's first satellite, marking the beginning of our space journey. It was built by ISRO and launched by the Soviet Union.

2008

Chandrayaan-1 - Moon Mission

India's first lunar probe discovered water molecules on the Moon's surface, making India the fourth country to place its flag on the lunar surface. The mission cost only $54 million, demonstrating cost-effective space exploration.

2014

Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan)

India became the first country to successfully reach Mars orbit on its first attempt, and at just $74 million, it was the most cost-effective Mars mission ever. It cost less than the Hollywood movie "Gravity"!

2017

Record 104 Satellites Launch

ISRO created history by launching 104 satellites in a single mission using PSLV-C37, setting a world record and showcasing India's capability in commercial satellite deployment.

2019

Chandrayaan-2 Mission

India's second lunar exploration mission aimed to land near the Moon's south pole. Though the lander faced challenges, the orbiter continues to provide valuable scientific data about the Moon.

2023

Chandrayaan-3 Success

India achieved a historic soft landing on the Moon's south pole, becoming the fourth country to land on the Moon and the first to successfully land near the lunar south pole. The mission demonstrated India's advanced space capabilities.

2023

Aditya L1 - Solar Mission

India's first dedicated solar mission to study the Sun from the L1 Lagrange point, providing continuous observations of the solar corona and space weather phenomena.

Ongoing

Future Missions

ISRO is planning Gaganyaan (human spaceflight), Venus mission, and Mars sample return mission, positioning India as a major space power for the future.
