Atoms for Peace and Progress

1948

Atomic Energy Commission

Established under Dr. Homi Bhabha, the Atomic Energy Commission laid the foundation for India's nuclear program with a vision of using atomic energy for peaceful purposes and national development.

1956

Apsara Research Reactor

India's first nuclear reactor, Apsara, became operational at BARC. It was Asia's first research reactor and marked the beginning of India's indigenous nuclear technology development.

1969

Tarapur Nuclear Power Station

India's first commercial nuclear power plant began operations, marking the country's entry into nuclear power generation for peaceful purposes and energy security.

1974

Pokhran-I (Buddha Smiles)

India's first nuclear test demonstrated indigenous nuclear capability. The test was conducted for peaceful purposes and established India's position in nuclear technology.

1998

Pokhran-II Tests

A series of five nuclear tests established India as a nuclear weapons state. The tests showcased advanced nuclear technology and strategic deterrence capabilities.

2002

Pressurized Heavy Water Reactor (PHWR)

India developed indigenous PHWR technology, enabling the use of natural uranium and establishing self-reliance in nuclear reactor design and construction.

2013

Kudankulam Nuclear Power Plant

India's largest nuclear power station with advanced VVER-1000 reactors, demonstrating international cooperation in peaceful nuclear technology.

2017

Advanced Heavy Water Reactor (AHWR)

BARC developed the innovative AHWR design, a thorium-based reactor that represents the future of clean nuclear energy and India's three-stage nuclear program.

Ongoing

Thorium Research Program

India leads global research in thorium-based nuclear technology, utilizing its vast thorium reserves for sustainable nuclear energy and reduced nuclear waste.
